  <h1>AI Sketchbook</h1>
  <p style="color: var(--ink-mid); max-width: 600px; margin: 0.75rem 0 0;">A working collection of ideas for using AI in humanities teaching and research. Not tutorials. More like notes passed between colleagues who have been thinking about the same problems.</p>
  <span class="section-accent default"></span>
</div>
{:/nomarkdown}

The humanities bring something specific to AI work: the habit of reading carefully, noticing what is absent, asking what a source cannot say. These sketches are built around that. But this is not a claim to authority. We are not presuming that we have the best answers, or even settled ones. In many cases we are still working out what responsible, useful, and intellectually serious AI use looks like in practice.

There is no shortage of AI advice online, and plenty of it is smart, generous, and worth reading. What matters to us here is something narrower and more local: building a community of practice around AI use at UNM, among people who share students, classrooms, institutional constraints, disciplinary habits, and campus culture. The point is not just to collect prompts. It is to make a space where faculty, students, and collaborators can compare notes honestly, test ideas in context, and help shape norms that make sense for this place.

Each entry has a status — *rough idea*, *tested*, or *refined* — that signals how much it has been pressure-tested. Most ideas here have not been through more than a few trials. Take them accordingly. Think of this less as a guidebook than as a set of working notes meant to support conversation, experimentation, and a shared local vocabulary for what thoughtful AI practice might become.
